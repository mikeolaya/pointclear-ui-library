Demographic

Age: 38

Occupation: Director of Digital Health

Archetype: The Health System

Overview:

Edward is the Director of Digital Health for a large health system operating across three large regions of two states. He oversees multiple innovative solutions such as mobile and devices that improve consumer outcomes and efficiencies. He is also responsible for expanding and implementing all things telehealth. The health system is fully EMR integrated; all workflows are organized within the EMR. This is the #1 thing he keeps in the back of his mind when integrating with technology solutions.

Edward finds telehealth compelling because it improves access to care, especially in rural areas, and lowers costs. A telehealth offering is a differentiator that makes their health system attractive to employers, which drives up membership. Increasing membership brings in more business. Telehealth is key to their population health strategy. Since they are reimbursed on a wellness basis, keeping patients healthy and out of the hospital by use of telehealth visits will drive their overall costs down.

There are many use cases for using telehealth. It will allow for the expansion of primary care, offering virtual options for follow-up visits. Recently discharged patients, especially those that cannot travel to a transitional medical clinic, can have telehealth visits with physicians or pharmacists to make sure they are following discharge instructions and properly taking their medication. Telehealth can also help with chronic care management, allowing patients to meet with mid-levels or their care team. He also wants to use telehealth for a nurse advice line and to meet with physicians for acute conditions. It is important to inform a patient’s PCP about these visits so they can follow-up if necessary. Telehealth can be used to improve physician-to-physician consults and multi-doctor encounters. Tele-ICU is also desired.

Key pain Points / Distractions

  • Physicians having to manage their schedules across multiple calendar systems
  • Not enough of their own providers on the platform. Health system providers not appearing first for acute care calls.
  • Disconnected digital offerings that do not part of the health system experience
  • Patients having to re-enter data
  • Disparate workflows
  • Waiting on new developments in the software

Primary Needs:

  • Ability for providers to synch their MDLIVE and EMR calendars for full visibility
  • Ensuring a single health system experience to patients across all their digital health solutions.
  • A deep data and workflow integration with their EMR
  • Better visibility to patient information and notes during sessions
