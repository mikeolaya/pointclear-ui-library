Chris Norton

The Medical Provider

"ER docs are a different breed. The ER is an ultimate example of throughput, getting people in and out as fast as possible."

Demographic

Age: 55

Occupation: General Practitioner

Archetype: The ER Doctor

Overview:

Dr. Norton has been a general practitioner, with a speciality in emergency medicine, for the past 24 years. The majority of his career has been in the ER, although he got worn down by the schedule and moved to an urgent care center a few years ago. He is a huge champion for patient safety and quality, and treating the patient so they are in and out as fast as possible.

A colleague of his recommended telehealth. Excited about what technology could do to solve some of the process problems existing in healthcare now, Dr. Norton explored the option of telehealth further. He has now been a telehealth provider for the past 2 years. Initially he had around 150 consultations a month, but is currently well into 400 - 600 consults. Now, Dr. Norton works mainly as a telehealth provider and works a few days a week at the urgent care center. He likes the flexibility telehealth provides, but still has some issues with the system.

Dr. Norton has a very active life and often takes consultations on the go. However, the platform does not allow him to see his availability on his existing calendar because the two calendars don’t synch. He holds most of his sessions via telephone because video takes more time to set up and the software is often an issue. Many video sessions are dropped and he spends too much time trying to reconnect with patients, impacting patient care and possibly causing him to miss out on potential revenue from other patients in need of assistance. He thinks telephone sessions are very effective and allows him to deliver the same level of care as video, so he is not compelled to make himself available for video sessions.

Key pain Points / Distractions

  • Inconsistent and unreliable video technology
  • Unable to synch their MDLIVE calendar with their existing calendars
  • No clear rules on how to conduct more personal consultations
  • Currently no easy way to refer a patient to a specialist
  • Receives messages when pharmacies do not receive prescriptions he sent
  • “I don’t want to have to worry about IT issues.”

Primary Needs:

  • Templates for common patient issues
  • Faster prescription submission
  • Alerts when new messages are received
  • Increase efficiency in automated phone communication from MDLIVE

Vanessa Kloss

The Medical Provider

“I want it to be easier for parents to find me so I can grow my pediatric patient base.”

Demographic

Age: 62

Occupation: Pediatrician

Archetype: The Pediatric Doctor

Overview:

Dr. Kloss is a pediatrician in Chicago, Illinois and has been practicing medicine for over 30 years. Currently, she works at a practice that she has been a part of for the last 10 years of her career. Dr. Kloss began working in telehealth two and a half years ago after being referred by another doctor. Now, Dr. Kloss sees telehealth patients in between her scheduled in-person patient visits. Eventually, she wants to retire from her brick and mortar practice, but keep telehealth as a way to continue practicing medicine as she transitions into retirement.

To maximize her reach and help build her patient base, Dr. Kloss works on three different telehealth systems, each with their own pros and cons. She finds that a majority of her issues have to do with her patients’ lack of tech savviness or reliable internet connections, especially with regard to video, uploading photos, and re-requesting her as a provider.

Aside from technical issues, Dr. Kloss has discovered that though she is a pediatric doctor, she has been lumped in with all of the other general practitioners on most of her telehealth platforms. This forces her to decline multiple adult patients daily. She feels that the platform should direct only pediatric patients to her and make it easier for parents to find her when their children are ill.

Key pain Points / Distractions

  • Inconsistent and unreliable video technology
  • Unable to synch their MDLIVE calendar with their existing calendars
  • No clear rules on what pediatricians can and cannot treat in terms of children
  • Currently has to decline adult patients that request her or end up in her queue
  • Cannot communicate with other providers on the platform

Primary Needs:

  • Templates for common patient issues
  • Custom pediatric settings, such as Top 30 Meds, since her patient needs are different
  • Need the ability to provide sick slips when necessary
  • Ability for patients to re-request her so a patient base can be developed

Emilia Mitchell

The Medical Provider

“I want Breakthrough to accept more insurance options so I can provide therapy to more people in need.”

Demographic

Age: 48

Occupation: Licensed Clinical Social Worker (LCSW)

Archetype: The Therapist

Overview:

Emilia is a licensed clinical social worker and has been practicing for 12 years. Working mainly in a private practice, Emilia had a great set of clients in her hometown of Sacramento, California. However, she recently found herself as a new resident to the Los Angeles area. Prior to her move, she was worried about losing her clients because of the distance. It was around this time that she was introduced to telehealth by an insurance company.

When Emilia moved she became a telehealth provider to allow her clients to continue seeing her. This allowed her patients to maintain their comfort level and stay with someone that knows them well, instead of starting over again with a new therapist. She was very happy to keep her client relationships and now spends a fair amount of time each week in telehealth sessions with new and existing clients.

Unfortunately, Emilia has noticed that there are not as many insurance companies listed on her current telehealth platform and feels that this is hindering the growth of her patient list. Having had a bit of trouble with marketing her practice in her new city, she is hoping that the inclusion of more insurance companies would make the burden of being both therapist and marketing team much easier.

Key pain Points / Distractions

  • Inconsistent and unreliable video technology
  • Unable to synch their MDLIVE calendar with their existing calendars
  • Currently no easy way to refer a patient to a specialist
  • Cannot communicate with other therapists on the platform
  • ICD-10 code fields are not smart and require the provider to know the specific codes they need

Primary Needs:

  • Increased number of insurance companies accepted on the platform
  • More appointment controls (in terms of making note of noshows and search capabilities)
  • Ability to set frequency of patient assessments
  • Better visibility to patient info during sessions in case of an emergency
