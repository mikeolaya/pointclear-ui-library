Maria Guerrero

The Medical Patient

“I’m always busy and need to the ability to get the care I need as quickly as possible.”

Demographic

Age: 36

Occupation: 3rd Grade Teacher

Archetype: The Medical Patient

Overview:

Ms. Guerrero works as a 3rd grade teacher in upstate New York. She spends her day keeping up with her class, then rushes home to take care of her family. She does not have time to go to the doctor when she feels under the weather. Unfortunately, she is having issues with what she thinks is a UTI. However, Ms. Guerrero is at work on a short lunch break and unable to leave to go to urgent care. It would be very inconvenient if she had to go to the urgent care after work and wait to be seen, even if it only took an hour. Who will pick up the kids? How will dinner get on the table? She and her family are heading out of town the next afternoon. and she can’t wait to take care of the issue later.

A coworker suggested talking to a doctor using telehealth. She was a little nervous, but felt with her tight schedule, this option would work best. The elementary school has horrible data service and blocks many websites, so she decides to call the service and set up an appointment that way.

Once she gets in touch with the operator and sets up an account, the operator helps her set up an appointment and connect with the first available provider. A provider calls her back within 2 minutes. The doctor was very friendly, helpful and was able to call in a prescription to her pharmacy that is on the way home. She found the entire experience convenient and will definitely use telehealth again. She really liked the doctor she spoke with and would like to see the same doctor in the future.

Key pain Points / Distractions

  • Gets first available doctor, but no idea of their background
  • A bad internet connection can negatively impact the experience
  • Setting up an account with an operator is easy, but takes some time. Setting up an account online would be faster.

Primary Needs:

  • Quick and easy access to a provider
  • Increase in number of insurance plans accepted
  • Would want the option of a follow up visit in case of faulty medication

Hayden Cooper

The Medical Patient

“I need the assistance of multiple providers and I need the process of that collaboration to be easy.”

Demographic

Age: 29

Occupation: Stay-athome Mom

Archetype: The Behavioral Patient

Overview:

Hayden is a 29 year old stay-at-home mom with a new baby of 3 months. After giving birth Hayden wasn’t like herself, fighting unusual thoughts and feeling like she was always walking through a fog. After doing some research online she thought it was time that she speak with a therapist. With such a young child and feeling some anxiety about leaving home, Hayden opted for telehealth for her therapy session. This medium would allow her to stay with her child and speak freely in the comfort of her home.

Hayden signed up for an account, with a bit of difficulty finding her therapist and finding a time for the appointment. She was only able to suggest times that worked for her instead of seeing what options her therapist of choice had available.

After a couple visits with her online therapist, Hayden was diagnosed with postpartum depression. At this point her therapist suggested that she speak with a psychiatrist, who would give her the option of medication to help her issue. Hayden wants to continue speaking with her therapist and wonders why the psychiatrist can’t join their recurring appointment to help and prescribe the medications. She doesn’t want to go through her whole story with another person or pay double for multiple appointments with two separate providers.

She really appreciates and feel that she needs the care she is being given by her provider, but wishes the collaboration between providers was a smoother process for her.

Key pain Points / Distractions

  • Inconsistent and unreliable video technology
  • Unable to clearly view the providers availability to schedule an appointment
  • Filtering options were unclear and hard to understand

Primary Needs:

  • Quick and easy access to a provider
  • Easy way of adding other specialists to the session if necessary
  • Increase in number of insurance plans accepted
  • Wants a clearer and easier messaging tool
  • Clear indicator of the status of their appointment
